Short answer

If you are searching for a Zety alternative no subscription, you probably want a resume builder that helps you finish and export without committing to an ongoing plan. The right alternative depends on what you need: guided writing, ATS checks, templates, PDF export, Word export, or no-watermark output. CV Desk is one strong iPhone option when you want ATS-minded resume building, pay-once access, and professional exports.

This page is not a negative claim about Zety or any other named product. It is a buying checklist for people who prefer a different pricing model or mobile workflow. Always compare current pricing and export limits directly on each product’s site or App Store page.

What to look for before choosing

  • Use a simple ATS-friendly layout: clear headings, normal text, reverse chronological experience, and no text hidden inside images.
  • Check whether the app can flag missing keywords, vague bullets, weak sections, or formatting problems.
  • Confirm export formats before paying: PDF for applications, Word or editable text for tailoring.
  • Avoid templates that look impressive but confuse parsers with columns, icons, or graphics.
  • Prefer no-watermark export so the final resume looks professional and belongs to you.

A practical decision process

  1. Paste the job description beside your resume: the best ATS check compares role language, not generic keywords.
  2. Fix structure first: contact information, summary, experience, education, skills, and projects should be easy to parse.
  3. Rewrite bullets with evidence: action, scope, tool, and measurable outcome when possible.
  4. Export and inspect: open the PDF and Word file to confirm spacing, links, and selectable text.
  5. Tailor per role: one excellent base resume still needs keyword and emphasis changes for each application.
